Predictive Maintenance of Hydraulic Systems

Problem: Predict hydraulic system failures using time-series sensor data.

Solution: Implemented ARIMA and SARIMA models for time-series forecasting and anomaly detection.

View Project Repository

Results: Using various sensor data points, we applied machine learning models to forecast potential failures. The SARIMA and ARIMA models helped predict time-series behavior, while the RandomForestClassifier identified the most important sensors. The system detected anomalies in pressure readings and offered insights into which sensors are most critical in predictive maintenance efforts.

Household Electric Power Consumption Analysis

Problem: Analyze household electric power consumption to find trends and insights over time.

Solution: We resampled the dataset by day, created visualizations using Power BI and Tableau to highlight trends, and conducted exploratory data analysis (EDA) and statistical analysis.

View Project Repository

Results: This project provided insights into energy consumption patterns over time, identifying peak usage periods and variations across the year.

Key Insights from the Analysis:

  • Global Active Power peaks during the summer and winter months.
  • Sub_metering_3 (AC and Water Systems) has the highest energy usage.
  • Voltage and Global Active Power show a consistent pattern over time.

Urban Air Quality Data Analysis

Problem: Analyze urban air quality data to understand the impact of pollutants over time and their correlation with environmental factors.

Solution: Implemented data cleaning, visualization, and regression models to identify trends in pollutant levels and their relationship with temperature, humidity, and traffic data.

View Project Repository

Results: By analyzing the air quality dataset, we visualized trends in pollutants such as NO2, CO, and PM2.5 over time. Using regression models, we examined the relationship between pollution levels and various factors such as weather conditions and time of day.

Top Features Considered:

  • NO2 Levels
  • CO Levels
  • PM2.5 Concentration
  • Temperature
  • Humidity
  • Wind Speed
  • Traffic Volume
  • Time of Day
  • Seasonal Variations

Visuals from the Project:

Findings:The heatmap shows strong positive correlations between temperature-related variables (e.g., tempmax, tempmin, temp) and the Health_Risk_Score, while wind-related variables (e.g., windspeed, windgust) have weaker correlations.

Findings:The temperature and humidity distributions are roughly normal, while the Health Risk Score is slightly skewed; the heatmap shows strong correlations between temperature variables and the Health Risk Score.

Findings:The scatter plot shows a relationship between temperature and Health Risk Score across various cities. Warmer temperatures generally correspond with higher health risk scores, with notable clustering patterns unique to each city.
